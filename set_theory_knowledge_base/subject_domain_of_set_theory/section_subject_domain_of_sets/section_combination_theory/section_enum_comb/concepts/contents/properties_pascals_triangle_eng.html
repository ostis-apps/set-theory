  • The sum of the numbers in the upward diagonal starting from the first element of the (n-1)-th row is the n-th Fibonacci number:

  • The sum of the numbers in the n-th row of the Pascal triangle is 2n.

  • All the numbers in the n-th row, except the ones, are divisible by n if and only if n is a prime number (a consequence of Lucas' theorem).

  • Each number in the triangle represents the number of ways to reach it from the top, by moving either right-down or left-down.
