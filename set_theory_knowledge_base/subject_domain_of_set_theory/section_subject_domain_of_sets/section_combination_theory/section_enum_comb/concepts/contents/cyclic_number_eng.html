A cyclic number is an integer where the cyclic permutations of its digits are products of that number with consecutive numbers. The most well-known example of such a number is 142857:

142857 × 1 = 142857

142857 × 2 = 285714

142857 × 3 = 428571

142857 × 4 = 571428

142857 × 5 = 714285

142857 × 6 = 857142
